Formats of Magic the Gathering


While Magic the Gathering has an extensive amount of cards, there are formats which restrict what cards you can use to keep games fair and organized. Magic the Gathering has cards divided into "sets" which correspond to when the card was printed.
Format name Description Banlist and additional information
Pauper Only cards printed at the rarity level of common can be used. The rarity ratings of Magic the Gathering are common, uncommon, rare, and mythic rare. Pauper information
Standard Only cards from the most recent 5 sets may be used Standard information
Modern Only cards printed in the set 8th edition and more recent can be used. Modern information
Legacy Cards from any set may be used Legacy information
Vintage legacy with a smaller banlist Vintage information
Sealed deck Given packs of cards, you must attempt to build a deck from only the cards opened in the packs given (and basic lands) NO BANLIST
Draft Each player is given a pack of cards, players select one card from the pack to use for deckbuilding then pass the remainder to the player on their left. Repeat this process until there are no remaining cards. Then using another pack of cards, repeat the selection process but pass to the right. For a third pack, repeat but passing left. Then players must use the cards they selected in this process to build a 40 card deck using these cards and any number of basic lands. NO BANLIST
Two headed Giant Players form teams of 2. Life totals become 30 to start the game. Players on each team take actions together. Decided among Pauper, Standard, Modern, Legacy, Vintage, or Commander
Commander Players choose a legendary creature or designated planeswalker to use as their "commander". Players use 99 card decks restricted to colorless cards and cards matching the commander's color identity (colors corresponding to the commander, in the ability text, and not in its flavor text or reminder text) Commander information
Kitchen Table Players may play with any cards that they have. Bans are determined by the group. This is a format meant to be as casual as possible, hence "kitchen table" Up to the players
